Department or Program : Taubman Health Sciences Library
School : University Library
Statement : Disability justice is at the root of all other social and political justice movements. Discrimination against a community begins with isolating them, othering them, and this typically takes the form of actions to imply that they are disabled. When we make disability inclusion the standard for human interactions, we take the wind out of the sails for so many other shapes stigma can take and help to protect other communities. Eliminating stigma and promoting inclusion and acceptance are core values and core actions for making life better for everyone. Ways to do this begin and center around being visible and creating opportunities for empathy and social bonding. To do this, we also have create and protect safe spaces for disabled individuals and communities, both in the physical world as well as in online and virtual spaces. Safety needs to be provided and protected for the body, mind, and spirit. These are aspirational goals, and I recognize that they can never be completely achieved, but I believe we have to keep trying.

Department or Program : Communication and Media
School : College of Literature, Science, & the Arts (LSA)
Statement : Much of what we conceptualize as problematic child media "addiction" is actually media use in the service of sensory regulation in an increasingly dysregulating built environment. My work is aimed at helping parents understand that their child's sensory needs are different from their own, and that children's (and adults') behavior need not be framed as "problematic" once its gratifications are understood. Inclusion involves understanding how built and other social environments accommodate some people more than others. Since sensory regulation must occur before learning can happen, inclusivity means recognizing and supporting the unique sensory-regulation needs of all learners.
